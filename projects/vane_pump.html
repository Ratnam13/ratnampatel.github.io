Dual-Lip Balanced Vane Pump — Simulation-Integrated Optimization

A reproducible pipeline linking cam-ring & port-groove geometry to a Multics lumped-parameter simulation and structured post-processing, enabling multi-objective optimization of volumetric efficiency and flow ripple.

Project Summary

This work replaces intuition-driven, prototype-heavy iteration with a computational design approach. A spline-based cam-ring parameterization and bounded groove-length scaling feed a coupled, lumped-parameter solver. Designs are evaluated across multiple operating conditions and progressed with a population-based optimizer to expose trade-offs between efficiency and ripple.

  • Affiliation: Maha Fluid Power Research Center, Purdue University
  • Focus: Pump performance via geometry & timing
  • Deliverable: Optimization-ready workflow + validated designs

Objectives

  • Improve volumetric efficiency
  • Reduce outlet flow ripple
  • Evaluate across multiple operating conditions
  • Use a formal, multi-objective optimizer

Method & Workflow

  1. Cam-ring parameterization: 12-point spline on the half-ring with variable radii and interior angles; C¹ continuity enforced, curvature changes bounded for manufacturability.
  2. Groove timing control: Inlet, primary-delivery, and compensation grooves scaled by angular span (start angle fixed) to tune port timing without altering depth/width.
  3. Simulation: Multics lumped-parameter model with fluid, forces, lubrication/contact sub-modules; reduced model used for optimization runs, full model for validation.
  4. Optimization: Multi-objective GA (MOGA-II) with feasibility filtering, variable bounds, and cross-condition scoring.
  5. Post-processing: Chamber volumes/pressures/flows extracted; metrics computed for efficiency and ripple; designs classified and compared to a reference.

Key Findings

  • Feasibility-guided filtering (displacement → geometry → angle/radius) sharply reduces simulation waste while preserving viable diversity.
  • Cam-ring curvature and groove timing jointly control pressure transitions, affecting both efficiency and ripple.
  • Representative optimized designs show measurable gains versus a reference under single- and multi-condition studies, with full-model validation used to verify trends.
