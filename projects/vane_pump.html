Hydraulic Vane Pump Optimization

Role: Graduate Research Assistant, Maha Fluid Power Research Center (Purdue)

Tools: CATIA V5, ANSYS Mechanical/Fluent, FreeFEM++, MATLAB, DAQ instrumentation

Context

Designed and optimized dual-lip vane pump components operating under high-pressure conditions. Objective was to reduce flow ripple and volumetric losses while maintaining structural integrity and manufacturability.

Approach

  • Modeled stator/cam-ring geometry in CATIA; applied GD&T and tolerance stack-ups.
  • Performed structural FEA for stress/deflection limits and CFD for leakage/flow oscillations; evaluated thermal-fluid coupling where relevant.
  • Designed DOEs in MATLAB to explore geometry and operating parameters; multi-objective trade-offs for ripple vs. efficiency.
  • Built and operated instrumented test rigs; integrated sensors and DAQ to correlate simulations with experimental data and diagnose failure modes.

Results

  • Up to 25.7% reduction in flow disturbance (ripple) vs. reference cam-ring geometry.
  • ~8% improvement in volumetric efficiency under targeted operating points.
  • Deliverables: validated design recommendations, regression test scripts, and documentation for reproducibility.
