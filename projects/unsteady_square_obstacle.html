Unsteady Flow Around a Square Obstacle

A high-fidelity simulation of two-dimensional incompressible unsteady flow around a square obstacle using stabilized finite element methods in FreeFEM++. The work focuses on the formulation, numerical stabilization, and physical interpretation of vortex shedding at moderate Reynolds numbers.

Background and Motivation

The flow past bluff bodies represents a canonical benchmark for testing numerical stabilization schemes. Unlike cylinder flows, the sharp edges of a square induce strong flow separation and unsteady recirculation zones, making it an ideal configuration to evaluate stabilization strategies such as Streamline-Upwind Petrov–Galerkin (SUPG), Pressure-Stabilizing Petrov–Galerkin (PSPG), and Least-Squares Incompressibility Constraint (LSIC).

The goal of the study was to construct a solver capable of capturing periodic vortex shedding and to extract characteristic parameters such as Strouhal number variation with Reynolds number.

Numerical Formulation

  • Governing equations: 2D incompressible unsteady Navier–Stokes, discretized via stabilized finite elements.
  • Weak form augmented by SUPG (advection), PSPG (pressure–velocity coupling), and LSIC (continuity stabilization).
  • Temporal discretization: second-order backward differentiation (BDF2).
  • Spatial discretization: triangular elements using Taylor–Hood (P2–P1) and P1b–P1 spaces.
  • Solver: semi-implicit scheme with sparse linear solver; τ-parameters scaled with local Reynolds number.

Results and Validation

The solver captures the onset of periodic vortex shedding for Reynolds numbers ranging from 50 to 400. Mesh-independence indicates ≈15k elements are sufficient for stable shedding frequency and pressure distribution. The Strouhal trends align with literature for bluff-body wakes.

Discussion and Insights

  • Stabilization efficacy: SUPG/PSPG suppress non-physical pressure oscillations while preserving convective accuracy.
  • Mesh effects: Spatial refinement primarily affects wavelength and pressure gradients; temporal refinement tunes phase accuracy.
  • Physical realism: Transition from steady to periodic behavior beyond Re ≈ 70, consistent with bluff-body theory.

Key Findings

  • Validated stabilized FEM formulation for advection-dominated incompressible flow.
  • Captured vortex-shedding frequency and wake asymmetry across increasing Reynolds numbers.
  • Demonstrated convergence of pressure and velocity fields across element orders and meshes.
  • Proposed a robust pipeline for Strouhal extraction from time-resolved fields in FreeFEM++.
