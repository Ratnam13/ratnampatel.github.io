Unsteady Flow Around a Square Obstacle

A high-fidelity simulation of two-dimensional incompressible unsteady flow around a square obstacle using stabilized finite element methods in FreeFEM++. The work focuses on the formulation, numerical stabilization, and physical interpretation of vortex shedding phenomena at moderate Reynolds numbers.

Background and Motivation

The flow past bluff bodies represents a canonical benchmark for testing numerical stabilization schemes. Unlike cylinder flows, the sharp edges of a square induce strong flow separation and unsteady recirculation zones, making it an ideal configuration to evaluate stabilization strategies such as Streamline-Upwind Petrov–Galerkin (SUPG), Pressure-Stabilizing Petrov–Galerkin (PSPG), and Least-Squares Incompressibility Constraint (LSIC).

The goal of the study was to construct a solver capable of capturing periodic vortex shedding and to extract characteristic parameters such as Strouhal number variation with Reynolds number.

Numerical Formulation

  • Governing equations: 2D incompressible unsteady Navier–Stokes, discretized via stabilized finite elements.
  • Weak form augmented by SUPG (for advection), PSPG (for pressure–velocity coupling), and LSIC (for continuity stabilization).
  • Temporal discretization: second-order backward differentiation (BDF2) for time accuracy.
  • Spatial discretization: triangular elements using Taylor–Hood (P2–P1) and P1b–P1 spaces.
  • Solver: semi-implicit scheme with sparse linear solver and adaptive τ-parameters based on local Reynolds scaling.

Results and Validation

The solver successfully captured the onset of periodic vortex shedding for Reynolds numbers ranging from 50 to 400. Validation against benchmark results confirmed accurate reproduction of wake frequency and pressure distribution. Mesh independence studies demonstrated that a 15 k-element mesh offered an optimal trade-off between accuracy and cost.

The computed Strouhal numbers showed expected scaling trends, and the element-order study revealed that low-order P1–P1 spaces exhibit pressure oscillations unless stabilization is applied.

Discussion and Insights

  • Stabilization efficacy: SUPG/PSPG schemes eliminate non-physical pressure oscillations while preserving convective accuracy.
  • Mesh effects: Spatial refinement primarily affects the wake’s wavelength and pressure gradients; temporal refinement influences phase accuracy.
  • Physical realism: Flow transitions from steady to periodic behavior beyond Re ≈ 70, matching theoretical predictions for bluff-body wakes.

Key Findings

  • Validated stabilized FEM formulation for advection-dominated incompressible flow.
  • Captured vortex shedding frequency and wake asymmetry across increasing Reynolds numbers.
  • Demonstrated convergence of the pressure and velocity fields across element orders and mesh refinements.
  • Proposed a framework for Strouhal number extraction in time-resolved FreeFEM++ outputs.

My Contributions

  • Implemented transient solvers and post-processing pipelines for pressure, velocity, and vorticity fields.

  • Documented solver stability and convergence properties in the final report.
