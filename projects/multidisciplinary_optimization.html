Multidisciplinary Design Optimization

A cohesive progression from gradient-based constrained optimization to derivative-free stochastic search and multi-objective Pareto analysis. I implemented and compared MATLAB solvers for structural and abstract design problems, then built trade studies with weighted-sum and ε-constraint methods.

A. Gradient-Based Constrained Optimization (Structural Beam)

I formulated a cantilever-like beam design with multiple inequality constraints capturing stress and lateral–torsional buckling limits, plus practical bounds for manufacturability (web/flange dimensions and span). B. Derivative-Free & Stochastic Optimization

For a nonconvex, bounded problem in four variables, I implemented a linear exterior-penalty reformulation and applied Nelder–Mead (MATLAB fminsearch), including multi-start experiments from diverse seeds. The code encapsulates the penalty-augmented objective and enforces symmetric bound constraints; runs were tabulated with function counts. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9} :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

I constructed a two-objective structural trade study and generated a Pareto frontier with the weighted-sum approach, validating coverage with an ε-constraint sweep.
