Mini Excavator — Pre-Compensated, Load-Sensing Hydraulics

System-level design and Simulink modeling of a pre-compensated circuit driven by a variable-displacement pump, supplying a boom cylinder and swing motor with load-sensing feedback to maintain a target Δp across the DCV.

Goal & Scope

Design a practical excavator circuit, size key components (cylinder/motor/pump), and implement a pre-compensated, load-sensing control in Simulink to examine flows, pressures, and power under representative tasks.

  • Architecture: Variable-displacement pump + compensator + DCV + actuators
  • Loads: Boom cylinder (sized from force/pressure), swing motor (gear-reduced)

Method

  1. Component sizing: Boom cylinder selected (≈4″ bore, 2.25″ rod, ≈27.4″ stroke) to meet force targets at ≤250 bar; swing motor sized from torque & gear ratio; pump displacement from combined peak flows.
  2. Modeling approach: Chambers via pressure build-up; DCV and compensator as variable orifices; compensator regulates Δp across the DCV using proportional control.
  3. Integration: Pump pressure from cumulative flows; separate subsystems for boom and swing; monitored flows/pressures and computed power/efficiency.

Outcome & Learning

  • All subsystems (orifice, chamber, DCV, compensator) behaved as expected in isolation.
  • The integrated model initially exhibited stiffness (Δt≈1e-11 s); migrating from mixed MATLAB Function blocks to Simulink primitives improved robustness; proportional control chosen for stability.
  • Next steps: refine initial states, solver/tolerances, and block optimizations for tractable whole-system runs.

My Contributions

  • End-to-end system architecture and component sizing from task/pressure specs.
  • Implemented load-sensing compensator and DCV control; migrated unstable custom code to Simulink blocks.
  • Set up power/efficiency accounting and diagnostics for solver behavior.
