CFD Analysis of Cyclone Separator Performance

Industrial cyclone CFD using hybrid meshes (hex-core, prism layers, tet connectors) with the Reynolds Stress Model (RSM) for swirling turbulence and a Discrete Phase Model (DPM) for particle tracking. I quantified how inlet geometry and orientation affect tangential/axial velocity fields and separation efficiency.

Overview & Context

Under the guidance of Dr. Ranjit S. Patil and Dr. Mahesh Dasar, I evaluated cyclone separator configurations to improve dust collection efficiency and flow stability. The work spans geometry creation, hybrid meshing in ANSYS ICEM, RSM turbulence modeling in Fluent, and Lagrangian particle tracking with DPM and the Discrete Random Walk (DRW) model.

Geometry & Hybrid Meshing

Baseline geometry followed the Lapple-type 2D2D proportions (cylindrical body + conical frustum). I generated hex-core cells in the main volume, prismatic boundary layers along walls for near-wall resolution, and tetrahedral connectors to transition between blocks. Mesh quality targets: orthogonal quality > 0.7 (95%+), skewness < 0.5, aspect ratio < 25.

Modeling Approach

  • Solver: Pressure-based steady state; coupled algorithm; Green-Gauss node-based gradients.
  • Turbulence: RSM to capture anisotropy and streamline curvature in swirling cores.
  • Near wall: Scalable wall functions; y+ within log-law region.
  • Particles: DPM with DRW dispersion; stochastic tracking to collection hopper/escape.

Inlet Geometry & Orientation Studies

1) Multi-Inlet: Rectangular vs. Trapezoidal

Rectangular multi-inlets produced stronger tangential velocity across radii 20‚Äì60 mm and steeper axial-velocity gradients near the wall, leading to higher overall collection efficiency. Trapezoidal inlets showed marginally better capture for sub-micron particles (‚âà0.3‚Äì1 ¬µm), likely due to smoother entry and reduced re-entrainment.

2) Trapezoidal-Inlet Orientation

Four orientations (90¬∞ steps) were assessed while keeping area constant. Orientation 1 (long parallel side tangential to the wall) delivered ‚âà20% higher tangential velocity (20‚Äì100 mm radii), higher axial core velocity, and the best collection efficiency. Orientation 2 showed unsteady characteristics and was excluded from efficiency comparisons.

3) Square-Section Cyclone (Validation)

A square-section variant was examined for manufacturability. Rectangular inlets again outperformed trapezoidal/circular counterparts; the square body did not offer efficiency gains vs the cylindrical baseline but confirmed trend consistency in the velocity field.

Key Results & Findings

  • Rectangular multi-inlets yield stronger swirl and higher overall efficiency.
  • Trapezoidal inlets can favor sub-micron capture but underperform for larger cut sizes.
  • Orientation matters: Orientation 1 maximizes swirl strength and collection.
  • RSM + DPM produced stable solutions aligned with literature trends for cyclone separation.
