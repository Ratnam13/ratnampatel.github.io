Axial Piston Pump — Kinematic & Fluid-Dynamic Model (MATLAB/Simulink)

Modular lumped-parameter model of a swash-plate pump: valve-plate overlap areas, chamber pressure build-up, and piston–cylinder leakage, assembled into an 11-piston machine and assessed for volumetric efficiency.

Context & Objectives

  • Model valve-plate port timing from geometry
  • Capture chamber dynamics & leakage mechanisms
  • Predict volumetric efficiency vs. speed/pressure

Assumptions and data: ISO VG-46 fluid, 40 cc/rev, 11 pistons, swash ≈19°, dead volume in chambers; image-based overlap areas for valve-plate; sinusoidal chamber volume curve.

Method

  1. Valve-plate area curves: Port overlap vs. piston angle computed from a CAD-style mask/intersection approach.
  2. Kinematics: Chamber volume curve with dead volume; piston stroke from geometry.
  3. Fluid blocks: Orifice flow; chamber pressure build-up using bulk modulus; leakage split into Poiseuille (Δp-driven) and Couette (piston velocity), with a fixed drain line reference.
  4. Assembly: 11 pistons with phase offsets; inlet/outlet port chambers; total flow & efficiency metrics.

Results (Representative)

Mean volumetric efficiency (ηv) across four operating points shows expected trends—lower at high outlet pressure; less deterioration at higher rpm due to shorter back-flow window.

  • 1000 rpm, 50 bar → ~98.9%
  • 1000 rpm, 400 bar → ~95.5%
  • 3000 rpm, 50 bar → ~99.3%
  • 3000 rpm, 400 bar → ~96.2%
