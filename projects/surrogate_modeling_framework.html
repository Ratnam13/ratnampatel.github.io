Surrogate Modeling and Optimization Framework

A three-stage framework covering design of experiments, surrogate model construction, and surrogate-assisted optimization. Implemented and validated in Python (NumPy, SciPy, SMT, and Pymoo) to illustrate the transition from sampling to adaptive design decisions.

Overview & Motivation

Surrogate modeling offers a tractable alternative to direct high-fidelity simulation. The framework developed here demonstrates sampling strategies, model building and validation, and ultimately surrogate-based optimization—key elements for reducing computational cost in complex engineering design (e.g., hydraulic-pump geometry optimization).

A. Sampling & Design of Experiments

Implemented and compared multiple sampling schemes: Full Factorial, Latin Hypercube Sampling, Halton, and Sobol Sequences. Evaluated uniformity using φₚ-criterion discrepancy and visual scatter for 1-D and 2-D test functions such as sin x/x and Branin. Demonstrated how low-discrepancy sequences improve uniform coverage with fewer points.

B. Surrogate Model Construction & Validation

Built surrogate models for analytical benchmarks (Currin, Branin, Himmelblau) using: Kriging (Gaussian process regression), Radial Basis Functions (RBF), and Polynomial Response Surfaces (PRS). Hyperparameters were optimized via maximum likelihood estimation. Models were compared via Leave-One-Out CV error, R², and visual residual plots.

  • LOOCV error reduced by > 90 % when switching from PRS to Kriging.
  • Correlation length in Kriging tuned via θ-optimization improves smoothness vs. overfitting.
  • RBF with cubic kernel yields robust interpolation across sparse designs.

C. Surrogate-Based Optimization & Adaptive Sampling

Extended the framework into optimization by integrating acquisition functions: Expected Improvement (EI) and Lower Confidence Bound (LCB). Used Pymoo’s sampling and constraint handling to balance exploitation–exploration on multi-modal surfaces (Currin, Branin). Demonstrated adaptive infill that iteratively refines promising regions, reducing true evaluations by ~70 % vs. uniform sampling.

Key Findings

  • Low-discrepancy DoE methods (Halton, Sobol) give best coverage for surrogate training.
  • Kriging provides superior accuracy for smooth, low-dimensional functions; RBF is robust for scattered data.
  • Adaptive infill with EI accelerates convergence toward global minima while reducing evaluations.
  • The full framework demonstrates an end-to-end surrogate-assisted optimization loop suitable for high-fidelity engineering workflows.
