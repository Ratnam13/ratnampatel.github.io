Surrogate Modeling and Optimization Framework

A three-stage framework covering design of experiments, surrogate model construction, and surrogate-assisted optimization. Implemented and validated in Python (NumPy, SciPy, SMT, and Pymoo) to illustrate the transition from sampling to adaptive design decisions.

Overview & Motivation

Surrogate modeling offers a tractable alternative to direct high-fidelity simulation. The framework developed here demonstrates sampling strategies, model building and validation, and ultimately surrogate-based optimization—key elements for reducing computational cost in complex engineering design (e.g., hydraulic-pump geometry optimization or airfoil redesign).

A. Sampling & Design of Experiments

Implemented and compared multiple sampling schemes: Full Factorial, Latin Hypercube Sampling, Halton, and Sobol Sequences. Uniformity was evaluated via φp-discrepancy and visual scatter for 1-D/2-D test functions. Low-discrepancy sequences consistently improved coverage for a fixed budget.

B. Surrogate Model Construction & Validation

Built surrogate models for analytical benchmarks (Currin, Branin, Himmelblau) using: Kriging (Gaussian process regression), Radial Basis Functions (RBF), and Polynomial Response Surfaces (PRS). Hyperparameters were tuned via maximum likelihood; models were compared via LOOCV error, R², and residual plots.

  • LOOCV error reduced by > 90% vs PRS when switching to Kriging on smooth functions.
  • RBF with cubic kernels remained robust on sparse, irregular designs.

C. Surrogate-Based Optimization & Adaptive Sampling

The optimization stage integrates acquisition functions— Expected Improvement (EI) and Lower Confidence Bound (LCB)— to guide adaptive infill. In the airfoil case, surrogates approximate CFD-derived drag responses and drive a design loop that balances exploration and exploitation.

Overall, adaptive infill reduced true evaluations by roughly 70% relative to uniform sampling, while the robust design step traded a small increase in mean \(C_d\) for a significant reduction in variance—valuable for reliability-minded applications.

Key Findings

  • Low-discrepancy DoE (Halton, Sobol) provides best coverage for a fixed sample budget.
  • Kriging delivers high accuracy on smooth, low-dimensional responses; RBF is resilient for scattered data.
  • EI/LCB-driven adaptive sampling accelerates convergence and cuts evaluations substantially.
  • Robust optimization reshapes the Pareto between mean performance and variability—often preferable to purely deterministic optima.
