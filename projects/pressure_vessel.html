Finite Element Analysis of Welded Pressure-Vessel Nozzle

Industrial FEA study of nozzle weld strength under agitator loading

Overview

This project was carried out during the BITS Pilani Practice School-I program at Viram Technologies Enterprises, Pune. The objective was to analyze the weld strength of a pressure-vessel nozzle subjected to agitator and pressure loads using Finite Element Analysis (FEA) in ANSYS Workbench. The 3D CAD model was developed in SolidWorks based on ASME Section VIII design data.

Simulation Setup

  • Material: AISI 316L (SA-240, SA-312, SA-182)
  • Design pressure: 3.2 bar (g)
  • Design temperature: 225 °C
  • Mesh: 95 k nodes / 47 k elements, tetrahedral, 16 mm near weld
  • Loads: Agitator weight 4009 kgf, torsional moment 1043 kg·m, internal pressure 3.2 bar
  • Constraints: Bottom face fixed axially & tangentially, free radially

Results & Verification

A mesh refinement study showed < 1.3 % variation in stress and < 0.2 % variation in deformation, confirming mesh convergence. Global equilibrium and local failure checks confirmed the structural integrity of the design. Maximum equivalent stress ≈ 24 MPa (< 109 MPa allowable), indicating a safe and robust design.

Conclusion

The pressure-vessel end design was found to safely sustain the applied agitator and internal loads without exceeding allowable stress limits. The study validated the nozzle-weld design under realistic service conditions in accordance with ASME Section VIII.
