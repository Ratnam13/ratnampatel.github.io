Hydrodynamic Lubrication & Bearing Modeling

A progressive study of thin-film lubrication that moves from planar validation to stepped, pivoted-pad, and journal bearing geometries. I built finite-difference solvers for the Reynolds equation, added Newton–Raphson updates for nonlinear coupling, and computed load, flow, friction, and temperature-rise metrics.

Project Summary

The work develops, verifies, and extends numerical solvers for hydrodynamic bearings. Starting with a flat slider for baseline validation, I then introduce a geometric step, a pivoted pad with variable film thickness and load balance, and finally a journal bearing. Each stage adds physics and numerical complexity while maintaining a consistent solver architecture and post-processing pipeline.

  • Theme: Hydrodynamic thin-film modeling (Reynolds equation)
  • Methods: Finite differences, Newton–Raphson updates, trapezoidal integration
  • Outputs: Pressure fields, load capacity, flow/side-leakage, friction, temperature rise

Methodological Progression

1) Flat Slider (Foundational Validation)

Baseline 1-D/2-D discretization of the Reynolds equation with constant film thickness. Used this stage to verify boundary conditions, grid convergence, and integration routines for load and flow before adding geometric features.

2) Step Bearing (2-D Reynolds with Step Interface)

Introduced a discrete step in film height and applied central differencing in the interior with one-sided differences at the step interface. Solved the nonlinear system with a Newton–Raphson update and integrated to obtain load capacity and inlet/outlet/side flows.

  • 2-D finite differences
  • Interface upwinding at the step
  • Load & flow comparisons

3) Pivoted-Pad Thrust Bearing (Variable Film + Load Balance)

Extended the solver to a pad with spatially varying film thickness and evaluated pressure, load, friction, temperature-rise indicators, and the load pivot location. Implemented a search for the pivot that yields zero net moment, and mapped metrics against the pivot position and film-thickness parameters.

  • Variable film geometry
  • Load-pivot search
  • Friction & temperature-rise indicators

4) Journal Bearing (Circumferential Pressure & Cavitation Handling)

Modeled circumferential pressure build-up in a journal bearing and examined side-leakage and cavitation boundary handling. This stage tested solver robustness on curved domains and highlighted the importance of boundary conditions and stabilization when shear-driven flow and pressure gradients coexist.

  • Curved domain discretization
  • Side-leakage evaluation
  • Cavitation boundary handling

Key Findings

  • Film geometry dominates load capacity. Step height, pad angle, and local curvature strongly influence peak pressures and integrated load.
  • Interface treatment matters. One-sided differences at geometric steps and careful residual linearization improve stability and convergence.
  • Pivoted pads exhibit clear trade-offs. The load pivot location correlates with friction, flow, and temperature-rise indicators, guiding practical pad positioning.
  • Cavitation boundaries affect integrals. In journal bearings, leakage and pressure plateaus are sensitive to how the sub-ambient region is modeled.
