Fluid–Acoustic Analysis of Flow Around a D-Shaped Cylinder

Undergraduate thesis — OpenFOAM LES simulation with acoustic post-processing

  • OpenFOAM (rhoPimpleFoam, LES)
  • Gmsh
  • ParaView
  • MATLAB

Objective

Investigate wake dynamics and associated acoustic signatures of a 2-D D-shaped cylinder in compressible flow using OpenFOAM with LES, followed by ParaView post-processing and frequency analysis in MATLAB.

Methodology

  • Geometry & Mesh: Gmsh layout with boundary-layer and wake refinement (≈193k nodes concentrated downstream).
  • Solver: rhoPimpleFoam (compressible), LES turbulence modeling; PIMPLE coupling; bounded Gauss schemes.
  • Boundary conditions: Uniform inlet velocity, wave-transmissive pressure outlet, no-slip cylinder wall, 2-D “empty” front/back.
  • Acoustics: Pressure probe in the wake; FFT of the signal to identify tones and estimate SPL.

Key Results

Using the probe signal, the dominant shedding tone was identified near 164 Hz. With the cylinder diameter and free-stream velocity, the corresponding Strouhal number was ≈0.27, consistent with canonical bluff-body wakes. Far-field prediction (e.g., Curle / Ffowcs-Williams–Hawkings) is outlined as future work to convert near-field pressure to radiated SPL.

Takeaways

  • LES captured coherent wake structures and their acoustic signature.
  • A single dominant shedding tone governs the spectrum; bandwidth reflects wake stability.
  • Next steps: implement FWH/Curle analogies for far-field sound and compare against reference cylinder data.
