Fluid–Acoustic Analysis of Flow Around a D-Shaped Cylinder

Undergraduate thesis — OpenFOAM LES simulation with acoustic post-processing

  • OpenFOAM (rhoPimpleFoam, LES)
  • Gmsh
  • ParaView
  • MATLAB

Objective

Investigate wake dynamics and associated acoustic signatures of a 2-D D-shaped cylinder in compressible flow using OpenFOAM with LES, followed by post-processing in ParaView and frequency analysis in MATLAB. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Methodology

  • Geometry & Mesh: Gmsh layout with refinement near the body; ≈193,560 nodes concentrated in the near-wake. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
  • Solver: rhoPimpleFoam (compressible), LES turbulence modeling; backward time scheme; Gauss-based spatial schemes. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • BCs (typical): Uniform inlet velocity; wave-transmissive pressure outlet; no-slip on the cylinder; 2-D “front/back” set to empty. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  • Acoustic probe & analysis: Pressure probe in the wake; FFT of signal in MATLAB to obtain dominant tones and SPL. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Key Results

The dominant shedding tone was identified near 164 Hz. Using the cylinder diameter and free-stream velocity, the computed Strouhal number was ≈0.271 (vs an assumed/benchmark value ≈0.238), consistent with periodic vortex shedding. Far-field methods (e.g., Ffowcs Williams–Hawkings / Curle analogies) were noted as next steps for radiation estimates. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Takeaways

  • LES captured coherent wake structures and their acoustic footprint.
  • Frequency content is dominated by a single shedding tone; SPL computed from band-passed signal.
  • Future work: implement FWH/Curle analogies for far-field sound prediction and compare with canonical cylinder data. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
